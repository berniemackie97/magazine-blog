## What you're building

A home lab for RE + red team work usually ends up as:

- **A safe playground** (intentionally vulnerable targets + isolated network)
- **A microscope** (RE tooling to inspect binaries, memory, traffic, and behavior)
- **A workshop** (compilers/IDEs/scripts to build POCs, harnesses, loaders, parsers)
- **A notebook** (notes + screenshots + "what I tried" logs you can reproduce later)

Keep the clean side and the dirty side separate. You will thank yourself later.

## Hygiene rules that save you pain

<CollapsibleSection
  title="Lab hygiene checklist"
  subtitle="Small habits that prevent big losses."
  label="Safety"
>
<Callout title="Lab hygiene">
  - Snapshot before you run unknown binaries.
  - Never reuse passwords between lab boxes and personal gear.
  - Do not upload unknown samples to public scanners without understanding the risk.
  - Write down what you did as you go. Future-you is a different person.
</Callout>
</CollapsibleSection>
